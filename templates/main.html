Dispatch Times Throughout San Francisco

Comparing dispatch times with location. Warmer colors signify longer dispatch times.

Which areas take the longest time to dispatch to?

And how can dispatch times be reduced?

            Based on the heatmap of dispatch times above, it appears that areas with the longest dispatch times are centered around the heart of San Francisco (specifically around Tenderloin, Union Square, and West Soma). Many of the longest dispatch responses occured on Market Street, Golden Gate Avenue, Ellis Street, and Eddy Street. Upon inspection of the street views in the area, it becomes quite evident that a major reason for these longer dispatch times is because of how few lanes there are and how densely the area is populated with shops, restaurants, residents, and vehicles. Obviously, these pose as obstacles to first-responders. One possible solution would be to reserve lanes for first-responders during times when emergecy calls are most frequent. However, these streets are congested as they are and this would only lead to an increase in traffic, likely causing public outrage. An alternative might be to invest in scooters or motorized bicycles (motorized because of the hilly S.F topography). Since these vehicles are much smaller and much more maneuverable, first responders would be able to reach their destinations much more quickly. Another noteable trend is that streets with longer dispatch times are mostly one-way streets, making these streets much harder to navigate. The impact of one-way streets is further supported through the examination of Eddy Street: West of the intersection between Eddy Street and Leavenworth Street, Eddy St. is a two-way street. East of the intersection, Eddy St. becomes a one-way street. Immediately, the frequency of longer dispatch responses increases. A solution to this problem would be to re-map traffic patterns into two-way streets. While this might be a nuisance to those already used to driving on one-way streets, it is a relatively cheap and simple solution to implement.
